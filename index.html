Hello there! My name is Ian Ruginski, and welcome to my small piece of the digital frontier. I am a cognitive scientist interested in applied perception, action, and spatial cognition research. I recently received my Ph.D in Psychology with a focus in Cognition and Neural Science from the University of Utah in May 2018. I made this website myself for free, deployed using GitHub via Netlify.

Specifically, my research focuses across two areas. The first focuses on individual differences in spatial cognition (such as people's ability to mentally rotate objects, which contributes to STEM field success) and navigation ability, including a consideration of mobility (how and how often we travel). Similarly, I am interested in the effects of emotion on execution of motor performance and perception. My second research area focuses on using cognitive theory to test and understand uncertainty visualizations(e.g. visually presented hurricane forecasts), with a focus on improving design for communication of risk to the general public.

My research methods include applied behavioral user testing, dynamical systems modeling, and virtual reality.
